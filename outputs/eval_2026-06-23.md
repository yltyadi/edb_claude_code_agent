## Key Differentiators

- **State.json streak language** is the single largest differentiator between v2 and v1: "EIBOR unchanged for 193 consecutive days" and Brent delta vs prior close are zero-cost to write once state.json is in place, but they transform the brief from a static snapshot into a temporal narrative that readers can orient in.
- **Corrected Operation 300bn method**: v2 uses the correct (200−133)/(300−133) = 40.1% gap-closed method vs v1's wrong 66.7% (200/300). This is a direct result of the CLAUDE.md patch appended after the June 22 eval.
- **All four calculations in every brief**: v2 includes all four required calculations (EIBOR, oil fiscal with AED, petrochemical AED figure, Op300bn run-rate) including the AED pass-through figure that v1 omitted. The CLAUDE.md feedback loop drove this.
- **[NEW] / [CONTINUING — N days] signal classification**: v2 distinguishes breaking news from carry-over signals at every mention — a discipline that prevents stale events from re-appearing as "breaking" in consecutive runs, and that is only possible with state.json's `signals_fired_last_run` and `last_run.date`.
- **EIBOR labeled in the calculation block itself**: Both v1 and v2 disclose the EIBOR estimation basis, but v2 places "EIBOR source: CBUAE live rate (June 19, 2026)" as the first line of the calc block — immediately visible to a credit analyst reviewing the output — whereas v1's disclosure is primarily in the methodology appendix.
- **Healthcare and Advanced Technology sector depth**: v2 provides concrete Healthcare specificity ("65% of medical devices sourced externally") and a clear Advanced Technology import-corridor angle; v1's Healthcare row is a placeholder and its AT row maps EIBOR rather than any technology-specific signal.
