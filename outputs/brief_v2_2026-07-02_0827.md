## Type A — Quantitative Signal Analysis

### A1. US Rates: 10-Year Treasury Surge

The 10-year US Treasury yield (DGS10) closed at **4.44%** on June 30, up **+6 bps** from 4.38% on June 29. This is the sharpest single-session move since the PCE 3.4% shock on June 25 and brings the 10-year to its highest level in this brief series. The move is consistent with the following transmission chain:

- Core PCE at 3.4% YoY (confirmed June 25, above 3.3% consensus) has not been absorbed by markets — the 10-year is repricing the "higher for longer" scenario.
- The 10-year breakeven inflation rate (T10YIE) moved in the opposite direction, falling 1 bp to **2.23%** on July 1 (from 2.24% on June 30). This divergence — nominal yields rising while breakevens fall — implies the real yield rose to approximately **2.21%** (4.44% − 2.23%), up from ~2.16% the prior session. Rising real yields tighten global financial conditions independently of the Fed Funds rate.
- CME FedWatch (per state.json) prices ~30% probability of a +25 bps hike at July 29 FOMC. The DGS10 move is consistent with that probability rising modestly.

**EDB transmission:** UAE sovereign bonds are priced off US Treasuries. A sustained 4.44% 10-year raises EDB's external borrowing cost and compresses the spread available for developmental lending. If the 10-year holds above 4.40% through July 29, EDB's next bond issuance window will face materially higher all-in costs than the prior issuance.

**Fed Funds context:** The FEDFUNDS series (FRED) shows 3.63% as of June 2026 — this is a monthly average and is 31 days stale. The effective target range remains 3.50–3.75% (5th consecutive hold, June 17 FOMC). The next decision is July 29, 2026.
